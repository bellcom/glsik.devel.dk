Working as pipe fitter in Denmark

You must be authorised to fit water pipes. Persons who have qualified as a plumbing fitter or smith in Denmark or in another EU/EEA country are permitted to fit water pipes. Such persons may work as a pipe fitter in Denmark, regardless of whether they are employed by an authorised plumbing contractor company.


Working temporarily or occasionally as pipe fitter in Denmark
Persons who intend to work on a temporarily or occasionally basis in Denmark as a pipe fitter on basis of their professional qualifications acquired in another EU or EEA country or countries with which the EU has entered into an agreement on access to pursue a regulated profession, are entitled to commence the work without notifying the Danish Safety Technology Authority.

Working permanently as pipe fitter in Denmark
Persons who intend to work on a permanent basis in Denmark as a pipe fitter on basis of their professional qualifications acquired in another EU or EEA country or countries with which the EU has entered into an agreement on access to pursue a regulated profession, have to fill out the form below to request for recognition of their professional qualifications.

Persons who intend to work on a permanent basis in Denmark as a pipefitter on basis of formal qualifications issued by a third country and who have three years of experience in the profession concerned on the territory of the Member State which recognized that the evidence of formal qualifications are in accordance with article 2(2) in the directive on the recognition of professional qualifications, have to fill out the form below to request for recognition of their professional qualifications.

Recognition of qualifications as pipe fitter
The Danish Safety Technology Authority recognises qualifications as a plumbing fitter or smith or similar training programmes completed in another EU or EEA country on equal footing with the Danish qualifications.

A course in fitting water pipes will also qualify individuals from another EU or EEA country to work as a pipe fitter in Denmark.

The content of the course or training programme must enable the pipe fitter to:

  • fit water pipes correctly to protect them from damage and provide the pipe fitter with knowledge of types of pipe
  • how to limit the spread of fire
  • documentation and suitable equipment. 

The course or training programme must also enable the pipe fitter to select pipe types and materials based on available documentation.

Furthermore, the Danish Safety Technology Authority will recognise a foreign citizen's qualifications as a pipe fitter based on relevant vocational experience according to Articles 16, 17 and 18 of the Directive on the recognition of professional qualifications.

The rules are laid down in Section 5- 8 of the Executive Order on the Running of Cables and Water Pipes (no. 649 of 31 May 2018). This defines who - in addition to an authorised plumbing contractor company - may fit water pipes without connecting and joining the pipes.

Pipes must always be connected by an authorised plumbing contractor company
Pipe fitters are not permitted to join water pipes and connect them.

When a pipe fitter fits water pipes, an authorised plumbing contractor company must always join, connect, inspect and test the installations.

The authorised plumbing contractor company that performs the connection work assumes liability for the entire installation towards the owner (user). This is stipulated in Section 7 of the Executive Order on the Running of Cables and Water Pipes.

The authorised plumbing contractor company may demand presentation of documentary evidence that the pipe fitter has completed a course or training programme and a statement to the effect that the pipe work has been completed according to the Danish rules.

Documentary evidence of qualification as a plumbing fitter or smith
If you wish to work as a pipe fitter in Denmark, you must be prepared for the fact that the plumbing contractor company will ask to see a copy of your certificate proving that you are a plumbing fitter or smith or a document that proves that you have completed a course or training programme. Therefore, you must have an English translation of your training certificate or documentary evidence for a course or training programme.
