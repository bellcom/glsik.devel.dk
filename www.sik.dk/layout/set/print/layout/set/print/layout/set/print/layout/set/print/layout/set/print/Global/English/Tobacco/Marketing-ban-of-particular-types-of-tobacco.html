Marketing ban of particular types of tobacco

It is banned to market tobacco, which is consumed orally. This ban includes e.g. snuff. As of 20 May 2020, it will be banned to market cigarettes and twist tobacco with a characteristic aroma such as menthol.


Cigarettes and twist tobacco with a characteristic aroma
It is banned to market cigarettes and twist tobacco with a characteristic aroma.

A characteristic aroma is a dominant scent and flavour of anything else than tobacco. Regarding cigarettes and twist tobacco with a characteristic aroma, which have reached a sales volume of 3 percent or above in the EU before 20 May 2016, the ban shall not have effect until 20 May 2020. Menthol flavour is an example of a characteristic aroma, which will be banned as of 20 May 2020.

Other kinds of tobacco
In Denmark, it is banned to market tobacco, which is consumed orally and which is intended for inhalation or chewing. E.g., snuff is covered by this ban. Moreover, tobacco, which includes certain additives, may not be marketed in Denmark.
