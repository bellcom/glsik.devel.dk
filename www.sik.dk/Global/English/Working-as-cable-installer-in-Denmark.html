Working as cable installer in Denmark

You must be authorised to run electrical cables when working in Denmark. Persons who have qualified as an electrician in Denmark or in another EU/EEA country are permitted to run electrical cables. Such persons may work as a cable installer in Denmark, regardless of whether they are employed by an authorised electrical contractor company.


Working temporarily or occasionally as cable installer in Denmark
Persons who intend to work on a temporarily or occasionally basis in Denmark as a cable installer on basis of their professional qualifications acquired in another EU or EEA country or countries with which the EU has entered into an agreement on access to pursue a regulated profession, are entitled to commence the work without notifying the Danish Safety Technology Authority.

Working permanently as cable installer in Denmark
Persons who intend to work on a permanent basis in Denmark as a cable installer on basis of their professional qualifications acquired in another EU or EEA country or countries with which the EU has entered into an agreement on access to pursue a regulated profession, have to fill out the form below to request for recognition of their professional qualifications.

Persons who intend to work on a permanent basis in Denmark as a cable installer on basis of formal qualifications issued by a third country and who have three years of experience in the profession concerned on the territory of the Member State which recognized that the evidence of formal qualifications are in accordance with article 2(2) in the directive on the recognition of professional qualifications, have to fill out the form below to request for recognition of their professional qualifications.

Recognition of qualifications as cable installer
The Danish Safety Technology Authority recognises electrician qualifications or similar training programmes completed in another EU or EEA country on equal footing with the Danish qualifications.

A course in running cables will also qualify individuals from another EU or EEA country to work as a cable installer in Denmark.

The content of the course or training programme must enable the cable installer to:

  • mount cables correctly to protect them from damage and provide the cable installer with knowledge of types of cable
  • how to limit the spread of fire
  • electrical documentation, including electrical drawings such as wiring diagrams and cable schedules, and suitable equipment. 

The course or training programme must also enable the cable installer to select cable types and materials based on available documentation.

Furthermore, the Danish Safety Technology Authority will recognise a foreign citizen's qualifications as a cable installer based on relevant vocational experience according to Articles 16, 17 and 18 of the Directive on the recognition of professional qualifications.

The rules are laid down in Section 1- 4 of the Executive Order on the Running of Cables and Water Pipes (no. 649 of 31. May 2018). This defines who - in addition to an authorised electrical contractor company - may run electrical cables without connecting and joining the cables.

Cables must always be connected by an authorised electrical contractor company
Cable installers are not permitted to join cables and connect them.

When a cable installer runs electrical cables, an authorised electrical contractor company must always join, connect, inspect and test the installations.

The authorised electrical contractor company that performs the connection work assumes liability for the entire installation towards the owner (user). This is stipulated in Section 3 of the Executive Order on the Running of Cables and Water Pipes.

The authorised electrical contractor company may demand presentation of documentary evidence that the cable installer has completed a course or training programme and a statement to the effect that the wiring has been completed according to the Danish rules. 

Documentary evidence of qualification as an electrician
If you wish to work as a cable installer in Denmark, you must be prepared for the fact that the electrical contractor company will ask to see a copy of your certificate proving that you are a qualified electrician or a document that proves that you have completed a course or training programme. Therefore, you must have an English translation of your training certificate or documentary evidence for a course or training programme.
